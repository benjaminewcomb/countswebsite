Homelessness

Ethical and pragmatic balance - use the law - strict enforcement obstructing sidewalk laws - arrest on first violation with mandatory treatment as alternative to incarceration. Potentially convert an old motel into drop in center for shower, meal, clothing. Possible match up of volunteers to individuals struggling as sponsor to solve individual cases.

Taking inspiration from the Mayor of San Jose who served two terms as a city commissioner before becoming mayor & is now running for governor of California. No need to reinvent the wheel - learn from those who have already found solutions to these challenges. San Jose leads the state of California in reducing the number of people living outside. San Jose has moved thousands of people indoors and over two-thirds remain indoors even years later while 911 and service calls for blight have decreased.

Please see link below to his interview which largely addresses solutions to homelessness.

San Jose Mayor Interview

Excerpts from interview

"Focus number of priorities to just several visible things people see in their daily lives like reducing homelessness, reducing crims with victims, cleaning up the streets and increasing accountability for delivering these outcomes. People care most about which will lead to meaningful progress"

"Cost of housing, cost of energy, quality public schools, quality of our safety in public spaces, the suffering of people who are deep in the throes of addiction or mental illness"

"Bring performance management into government and set goals around a few really important things, set some priorities and measure every dollar we spend and hour of staff time to validate that dollar or hour of staff time is actually moving us closer to the goals we have."

"Government is struggling to turn tax dollars into impact because of how bureaucratic, process heavy & litigious the city is"

"Processes & litigation have led to a system that can't execute anymore"

" Laser focused on bringing people indoors. First barrier is there are not enough beds for people. Pursue the fastest, cheapest, dignified & ethical way to add a lot of beds & give people a real alternative like buying sleeping cabins - these prefabricated modular units & building them into tiny home communities on public land. Buying old motels & converting them into transitional housing. Individual shelters with their own room with a door that locks to give privacy & safety."

"A confluence of drug & mental health crises, poor choices & bad luck thats going on that needs to be addressed"

" Inpatient facilities for folds who are currently on the streets or safe, dignified shelter. Certainly not be allowed to just choose to live outside because you're trapped in a cycle of addiction. Thats not a very compassionate or progressive position to just let people endlessly cycle and ultimately die on the streets."

"Build a lot of shelter, incentivize & require that people come indoors, cannot be allowed to choose to camp in a public space when being given a dignified low barrier alternative."

" City employees feel like their job is to sit behind a desk, manage process, manage regulation, and decrease legal risk versus actually being held accountable for delivering an outcome."

" Align budget & staffing to achieve goal, change how we operate & create accountability for outcomes."

"Make opportunity & upward & social mobility available"

"It's a strange notion that what should be primary is everyone's right to occupy any public space because they are a citizen of this state & in the case of someone whose addicted to drugs or mentally ill & homeless just to let them live out the chaos of their life on the sidewalk, to let that proceed is clearly not compassion, it is some idiot form of compassion to think that simply not interfering on someone's freedom while their life unravels on the corner is high ethical integrity. Demand that people be put in shelter or receive treatment because everyone is paying an enormous price economically & psychologically for the unraveling of social fabric in this way."

"A person deep in the throes of addiction to something like meth or fentanyl or a severe mental illness does not really have much liberty even if they are roaming the streets. And it is a huge harm to the liberty of others."

"Mental health courts would give a judge authority to mandate treatment."

"Even if your going to say well theres really kind of nothing wrong with people just choosing to waste their lives in the throes of addiction & if they want to just use until they ultimately die thats their choice but the question is, what is your impact on others"

"Convert a motel with 50-75 rooms or build these pre-fabricated modular units on public land with up to 200 units. Enhance blight eradication, enhance police patrols - blight control, increased police, decreased trash, no homelessness, no tents, no trash."

"Prioritize moving indoors people who are homeless in a neighborhood."

" Strictly enforced no camping zone around the site in a radius to guarantee the neighborhood will be made better, not worse off, by taking on a solution. Philosophically that is where we have to go acknowledging during implementation, will not be perfect."

"Move people from unmanaged encampments without rules to a site with security, case management, meals, some structure & some privacy & it changes the game for these people, changes entire possibility of actually escaping this miserable condition."

"Outcomes based management public budgets"

"Tack spending to performance and be clear about outcomes we should be delivering"

"Incentivize truly solving the problem being addressed"

"Bring a performance mindset into government, to everything we do so that we understand the value of a dollar we spend."

"Mississippi has been able to get more than 90% of their third grade readers on grade level for reading by mandating use of phonix in reading teaching."

"Want to make government work, align city employee choices & spending with things that work."

"Profit center for lawyers compounded by regulations"

"Thousands of these kinds of things in the way that the state or city works & in the regulatory environment that creates great costs & leads to overlitiginous environment"

"The market can work"

"Putting progressive values into practice and proving that they work when they come into contact with reality."

"Government that works"

"Allow DA to bring charges & a judge to ultimately sentence with a treatment mandated felony - like your using meth in the park and the kids can no longer use the playground - you can be given a choice between treatment & incarceration. So it brings consequence, accountability back acknowledging the immense societal impacts of things like public drug use that then often lead to trespassing, vandalism, retail theft & all these other impacts."

"Prioritize spending in the budge to build/operate the centers and require at a minimum people come indoors into a safe environment. Getting people into drug courts & making those drug courts work. You can't just use drugs in public endlessly, you've got to go into treatment."

"You have a republic, if you can keep it"

"Fallen into this false assumption that we can just focus on our personal lives, our families & our friends, our social & professional lives, build a career & figure out how to make money & not invest in this third space of life that is our public & civic life."

"Robust civic life"

"The system is in need of serious reform"

"Goal should not be incarceration unless someones really harming others"

"Goal should always be the least coercive, most life-affirming path but we actually do have to intervene sometimes & try to get people on that path, cannot just leave them to endless cycle of sleeping & dying on the streets."

"Using drug courts & mental health courts giving a judge the authority to mandate treatment."

"Lack of treatment capacity, & treatment beds for addiction and mental illness."

"Fentanyl & meth are much more potent, widely available, cheaper & accessible than heroin was in the 80s so theres a whole real crisis around that"

"The very real cost of crime and dysfunction playing out in front of everyones eyes"

"Focusing on the easy target versus solving the bigger more structural issues that really matter for people"

"Addressing the basics - adequately enforcing laws, maintaining & cleaning up safe/accessible public space, enabling our children to read in elementary school"

"Quick and efficient permitting"

"You think about the most basic things a functioning society has to provide, we're really struggling to deliver on stuff that ought to be very simple"
