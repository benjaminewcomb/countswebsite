Safety by prioritizing resources to protect against crimes with victims

Prioritize resources to protect against violent offenders & crimes with victims (including obstructing sidewalks) but decriminalizing open container, marijuana, paraphenelia, underage drinking, fake ids -> all these things can be dealt with via citations. Need police to focus on scary people, not so much on the young/old/non-threatening. Do not want to build criminal records for our youth for partying when there is plenty of dangerous crime to deal with in Daytona.

Marijuana possession ordinance removed from criminal offense to citation. If things improve, consider removing possession other drugs from criminal to citation or offer treatment instead of incarceration in cases of meth/ fentanyl/ heroin/ addiction.

Decriminalization non-violent offenses. Grace for the young and drunk, bring back the drunk tank. Consider seperate enforcement team for drunks vs dangerous/violent encounters. All the young arrested for relatively benign charges will have bad impressions of police going forward.

Consider providing the homeless with STI testing, contraception like NuvaRing or incentives for sterilization PRN.

Confidential fentanyl test strips & Narcane at Seabreeze, Beach street, Ridgewood and soup kitchens.

Safe work places for exotic dancers, maybe tip jars instead of having to get close to male patrons.

Not have only sheriff able to issue citations for dogs on the beach, delegate to lifeguards and citizens, citizen's citations would free up resources and increase enforcement.

Remove criminal penalties for selling sex, not for buying. Target buyers/exploiters, not sellers. Problem-solving courts for prostitution related charges with resulting mandated service or substance use treatment or job training or counseling or education pathways. Arrest focus modes create criminal records and push activity further under hiding. Want cops giving most of their attention to dangerous/violent criminals, not these hard up women (although we also do not want riff raff on our streets).

So far the police chief's beachside strategic plan looks good/effective & is improving safety in parks. Do not recommend closing properties however for a year that have been involved in drug house nuisance activity as vacancy will likely worsen problem. Deregulation, getting rid of special uses permit requirements and decreased zoning will decrease vacancy which will decrease blight and crime through things like community engagement and lighting both highlighted as ways to decrease crime in police chief's report on beachside crime unit. If combined with decriminalization & deregulation would promote expansion bicycle unit. Escalating penalties for open air narcotic use for these repeat offenders.
