Volusia Forever / development

Volusia Forever provides funds for county acquisitions of environmentally sensitive lands. Volusia Forever had 61% voter approval in 2000 and 76% voter approval in 2020. Chair of the County Council posted the following questions about Volusia Forever to Facebook. Please find my responses below.

No, to prevent money from going to bail out well-connected landowners (there has been some high price/acre paid from that fund) recommend ownership go to the people whose money is buying the land.

No, instead of the county owning the land, the citizen's whose money purchased the land should own the land. This could be in the form of a trust for the community. Pool citizen's money together to promote conservation.

No, keep government out of the equation. Private citizens will have more money with less taxes and they can decide how to use it.

Regarding suggestions to preserve the integrity of VF - Of course for the majority of voters who are fine with the government being the tool to pool money to protect these lands - that is fine. Forever generally means forever, however voters who do not want their money going towards this (more than 20% in 2020) should not have their money going to something they do not support.

No, approach to protecting the wetlands is to protect them from the government messing them/their protection up. However using already collected tax money, since it is not getting sent back to the people, is fine to use towards protecting our wetlands so long as it goes to something that can be argued to benefit the most tax payers.

No, there are not development applications that I would like to see slowed down - my votes would be yes on development with intention to restore tort law to prominence to deal with any issues that might arise. Do not feel the surrounding neighborhoods have a voice in the plans for someone else's private property. Their voice is their money & they can pool their money to contract easements & stipulations to block things they do not want done or to buy property outright. But if they do not, it is not on the property owner to be hamstrung, waiting to do something with the property they own.
