Daytona Beach Flood Control Study, FL Investigations

Congressional Districts: 6, 7.

This study was authorized by Section 1201(11) of the Water Resources Development Act of 2016 and is focused on flood risk management for Daytona Beach in Volusia County, Florida.

The current study area is a 2.1-square-mile area bordered by Nova Road, Orange Avenue, Ridgewood Avenue, and Beville Road. This Area of Concern includes about 8,000 residents and 2,800 structures, where chronic flooding has caused up to $80 million in property damage.

The area drains toward the Halifax River through Nova Canal. Flooding is worsened by low-lying bowl-shaped topography, runoff entering from Navy Canal, and tide conditions that can reverse Nova Canal flow and push water back into neighborhoods.

The project summary states that flood risk reduction is needed because flooding is recurring due to low elevation, high-tide backflow, and inflows that exceed the current Nova Canal design capacity.

Study Status

On January 22, 2024, the Federal Cost Share Agreement was signed. A government-only charrette occurred on March 14, 2024, and an Alternative Milestones Meeting occurred on May 22, 2024.

A Vertical Team Alignment Memorandum (VTAM) was signed by the Major Subordinate Command on August 18, 2025, and was submitted to the Assistant Secretary of the Army for review and authorization.

The aligned scope, schedule, and budget shifted based on the selected course of action. The updated study budget is $4.2M.

The study is currently 100% federally funded through the Disaster Relief Supplemental Appropriations Act, 2023. An additional $1.2M is estimated to be needed, primarily for complex scenario modeling.

If additional funds are not obtained, Article II of the Feasibility Cost Sharing Agreement (executed January 22, 2024) specifies that the study must be suspended upon exhaustion of funds.
