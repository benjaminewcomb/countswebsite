Sunshine Laws

Amend Sunshine Laws so city commissioners - only at the city level, no higher - are able to communicate outside of in-person public meetings as long as the communication in its entirety is in a public forum, is recorded, and can be accessed by the public at a later date. For example, via Substack or Discord. Each constituent of a city can be made an account & could fully engage in the discussion with the commissioners. This would enable constant analysis of decisions and create a feed-back loop between commissioners and citizens.

How can the commissioners make knowledgeable decisions when they have only five days to go through agendas which are often over a thousand pages, and only one meeting to assess and decide on the agenda items in their entirety.

This would be a process-oriented change that would improve the functionality of the decision-making process. The current law prevents back and forth discussions outside formal meetings and makes it harder to negotiate, collaborate or workshop ideas efficiently. The current law makes governing slower and more rigid. Some flexibility would help commissioners work through issues with all concerned parties, including the people, before formal votes.

It would be a more formal and cleaner forum than Facebook where current commissioners make an effort at engagement because only proven constituents would be made accounts. It would be better for the citizens because often on Facebook communication with the commissioners is limited - anyone can comment and no one can send messages to commissioners.

An online designated forum for discussion of city matters would help solve the problem of public comments not allowing for much back and forth with commissioners, solve time constraints so meetings do not have to run until after midnight, and provide for reliable communication. Often emails to commissioners go unanswered or only an automated email is received. It can be difficult to get calls through or to leave voicemails. With contact forms on websites there is no record of them being sent on the sender's side and typically do not receive a reply. Regarding open door policies, at city hall the only person in front of the closed door is the receptionist, you are encouraged to make an appointment, and city employees are often, understandably, unavailable.

An online forum via Substack or Discord would be at the 24/7 convenience of the commissioners and the citizens. It would also reduce the sense of haste and unexpected decisions at commission meetings, as discussion and decision-making would no longer be compressed into a single session. Recognizing some agenda items receive first and second readings and that commissioners have access to materials in advance, creating more space for open discussion can only lead to better-informed decisions.
